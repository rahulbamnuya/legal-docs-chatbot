Title: Basic Citizens' Rights and Traffic Laws

1. Every citizen has the right to freedom of speech, religion, and peaceful assembly under Article 19 of the Constitution.

2. During police encounters, you are allowed to ask for the officer's identification and badge number.

3. If you are arrested, you have the right to remain silent and ask for a lawyer immediately.

4. Wearing a helmet is mandatory for two-wheeler riders under Motor Vehicle Act Section 129.

5. Running a red light or using a mobile phone while driving can result in a fine or license suspension.

6. Pedestrians have the right of way at zebra crossings. Vehicles must stop when pedestrians are crossing.

7. The legal age for driving a two-wheeler without gear is 16 years, with a valid license.

8. Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs is a punishable offense with imprisonment or a heavy fine.

9. Using seat belts is compulsory for both the driver and front-seat passenger.

10. Traffic violations may lead to penalty points on your license, which can lead to suspension.

Always be aware of your legal rights and responsibilities.
